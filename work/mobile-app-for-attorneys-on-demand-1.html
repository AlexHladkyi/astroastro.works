Mobile app for Attorneys on Demand

About the project

Designs for a mobile app for Attorneys On Demand, the first national court appearance service that provides hearing coverage across the US.

The challenge was in bringing the service's main features of a primarily web-based service to mobile and work alongside it. The app would present relevant cases to attorneys, notify them if the cases they have accepted were updated or canceled, and allow them to submit court hearing results.

My role included:

– User experience (UX)
– User flows
– Wireframes
– Prototypes
– User interface (UI)

New cases and case details

New cases get placed on the Available Cases screen. Case details show additional information about a given case. Primary actions appear at the bottom of the screen for easier reachability and one-handed use.

Updated case alerts

When an accepted case gets updated or cancelled, it shows up on the Updates screen. Updated information appears highlighted until the attorney accepts the changes.

My Cases

Cases accepted by an attorney get moved to the My Cases screen.

Completing cases

When completing a case, attorneys add notes and attach photos of the relevant documents. To go with the principle of invaluable user input, anything that an attorney types or attaches is stored until the case is complete.
